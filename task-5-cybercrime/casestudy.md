## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into approving fraudulent payment requests or sharing sensitive information. In a typical scenario, a college student receives a phone call or message claiming to be from a bank or payment service. The attacker asks the user to “verify” their account by sharing an OTP or by approving a UPI collect request.

The process usually involves social engineering. First, the attacker builds trust by using official-sounding language. Next, they send a payment request instead of a payment and instruct the user to approve it. Once the user enters their UPI PIN, money is transferred from the victim’s account to the attacker.

Students and new digital users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering funds.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful verification before performing any financial transaction online.
